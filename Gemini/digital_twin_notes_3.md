The primary way to interact with and manage Agentic AI teams is through integrations, custom-built assistants, and Slack's native AI features.

1. Assigning Tasks and Projects (Control)
You control the agents primarily through @mentions and slash commands in a team-based, conversational environment.

Conversational Task Assignment:

@Mention the Agent: You can bring an AI Agent into a project channel or a direct message just like you would a human teammate.

Example: In the #product-launch channel, you could type: @RFP-Agent, please generate a competitive intelligence report for the 'Apollo' project and summarize the key risks in a thread.

Goal-Oriented Instructions: Agentic AI is designed to be goal-oriented, meaning you assign the outcome, and the agent autonomously plans and executes the necessary steps across different systems (e.g., pulling data from Salesforce, drafting a document in Google Drive).

Slash Commands and Workflow Builder:

/commands: Many integrated AI assistants will have specific slash commands (e.g., /project-agent onboard-new-member) to trigger complex, predefined workflows.

Workflow Builder: For routine tasks, you can use Slack's Workflow Builder to create custom, low-code processes that kick off an agent's task when a specific event happens (e.g., "When a message in #support-tickets is tagged with 'Urgent,' kick off the @Triage-Agent").

2. Monitoring Progress and Status (Visibility)
Slack's conversational interface makes monitoring highly contextual and real-time.

Real-Time Updates: Agents provide updates directly in the channel or thread where the task was assigned.

Status Reports: They can post automated updates on their progress (e.g., @RFP-Agent: 50% complete. Data extraction phase finished, now starting risk analysis.)

Action Notifications: Agents can notify human team members when a task requires human input, a decision, or approval.

Summaries and Recaps (Slack AI):

Slack's built-in AI features (available on various paid plans) can summarize long threads or entire channels. This is crucial for human managers to quickly catch up on the AI team's progress without reading hundreds of messages.

Example: You can ask Slackbot: @Slackbot, what are the key action items the RFP-Agent has completed in #product-launch this week?

External Dashboards via Integrations: For deep project management, the Agent will often post key metrics or links to external dashboards (like in Jira, Asana, or a custom analytics tool) back into the Slack channel for quick reference.
